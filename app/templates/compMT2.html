Mini-Tourney #2: Black or White

Basic Information

Theme: Black or White
Event Date: Saturday, 25th February 2023, 4-7pm

Description

One way of classifying any rhythm games stepchart is by classifying it into "black" chart or "white" chart.

"Black" chart, also known as specialist chart, requires less effort to clear for some people and a lot more effort to clear for some other people. These charts require very specific skills that are usually not found normally in many other charts. In PIU context, footswitches, jacks, gimmicks or brackets can cause a chart to be classified as "black" chart.

"White" chart, on the other hand, is a very straightforward chart that would usually require skills that are commonly found in many other charts. The charts do not play tricks on the players and it should be very easy to sightread "white" charts. In PIU context, a heavy run/stamina or twisty chart would usually be classified as "white" chart.

Players usually do not debate so much about how easy or difficult a "white" chart is, but there will be heated discussions on how easy or difficult a "black" chart is. Read more about "black" vs "white" chart in my blog here.

Scoring System

We will use EX-Score system in this mini-tourney. The calculation is done as follows:

EX-Score = (perfect + 0.8*great + 0.5*good + 0.1*bad) / (perfect + great + good + bad + miss)

Tourney Mechanics

Round 1

Round 1 is a singles round. Players will be paired up randomly and will compete against each other.

In each pair,

  • Players will do rock-paper-scissors
  • One player can decide between Black or White
  • The other player can decide between Low or High
  • A song will be randomised from the list of handpicked charts

Player with higher score wins and proceeds to the next round

Round 2

Round 2 is a doubles round. Winners from Round 1 will be paired up randomly and will compete against each other.

In each pair,

  • Players will do rock-paper-scissors
  • One player can decide between Black or White
  • The other player can decide between Low or High
  • A song will be randomised from the list of handpicked charts

Player with higher score wins and proceeds to the final round

Final Round

Round 3 is a UCS round. Winners from Round 2 (can be more than 2) will play both a Black UCS and a White UCS that has been prepared beforehand.

  • The winners will do rock-paper-scissors (or any other random games) to decide the order of play.
  • The first player will have to sightread the chart, but allowed to choose between Black or White.
  • After all players play one UCS, the order will be reversed for the other UCS.

Player with highest total score of Black UCS + White UCS wins
